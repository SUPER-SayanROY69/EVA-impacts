Srinagar - the breeze feels off

Srinagar is home to flavorful Kashmiri cuisine, known for its rich, aromatic dishes influenced by Mughal and Persian flavors. Popular dishes include Rogan Josh, Yakhni, Modur Pulao, Dum Aloo, Goshtaba, Nadru Monje, Sheermal, and Kahwa.

Srisailam - Andhra Spicy Meals

Srisailam, a sacred town in Andhra Pradesh, offers traditional South Indian cuisine with bold spices and temple prasadam. Must-try dishes include Pulihora, Mirchi Bajji, Gongura Pachadi, Andhra Chicken Curry, Pesarattu, Bobbatlu, Gutti Vankaya, and Ragi Sangati.

Darjeeling - Tea and Tibetan Delights in the Hills

Darjeeling’s food scene blends Tibetan, Nepali, and Bengali flavors, perfectly complementing its scenic hills. Some must-try dishes are Thukpa, Momos, Aloo Dum, Sel Roti, Churpee, Gundruk, Phambi, and Darjeeling Tea.

Nepal - A Taste of the Himalayas

Nepalese cuisine is a mix of Tibetan, Indian, and Newari influences, offering wholesome and flavorful dishes. Some famous foods include Momos, Dal Bhat, Sel Roti, Chatamari, Yomari, Thukpa, Gundruk, and Sekuwa.

Vishakapatnam - Coastal Spices and Seafood Wonders

The coastal city of Visakhapatnam is known for its spicy and seafood-rich Andhra cuisine. Popular delicacies include Royyala Iguru, Bongulo Chicken, Chepala Pulusu, Pootharekulu, Rava Dosa, Kodi Pulao, Gongura Mutton, and Karam Dosa.

Udaipur - Royal Platters of the City of Lakes

Udaipur’s royal Rajasthani cuisine is rich in flavors, spices, and traditional cooking methods. Must-try dishes include Dal Baati Churma, Gatte ki Sabzi, Laal Maas, Mohan Maas, Ker Sangri, Mirchi Vada, Ghewar, and Mawa Kachori.
